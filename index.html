— FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

How long is the program?

+
Our dental assisting program is designed to be completed in [X months], combining classroom instruction with hands-on clinical training so you graduate fully prepared.

What is the cost of the program?

+
Tuition is [$X,XXX], which covers instructional materials, lab supplies, and exam fees. Our admissions team can walk you through current pricing and payment options.

What certificates do I graduate with after this course?

+
Graduates receive a Certificate of Completion in Dental Assisting, and the curriculum prepares you to sit for relevant national certification exams.

What are the basic admission requirements?

+
Applicants must be at least 18 years old and hold a high school diploma or GED. No prior dental experience is required to apply.

Does the school find us an externship office to complete hours?

+
Yes — we partner with a network of local dental offices and help match you with an externship site that fits your schedule and location.

Do you find us a job when we complete the course?

+
While we can't guarantee employment, our career services team actively connects graduates with hiring dental practices and supports your job search every step of the way.

Do you offer weekend or evening classes?

+
Yes, we offer flexible scheduling — including evening and weekend classes — so you can train around your existing work or family commitments.

What kind of job placement assistance do you offer?

+
We provide resume building, interview preparation, and direct introductions to local dental offices that are actively hiring our graduates.

Is financial aid available?

+
We offer several financing options, and our admissions advisors can help you determine if you qualify for financial aid or a payment plan.

Do I need prior experience to enroll?

+
No prior dental experience is necessary. Our program is designed for beginners and covers everything from the fundamentals up.

What is the job outlook for dental assistants?

+
Dental assisting is a growing field, with steady demand for trained assistants across private practices, clinics, and specialty offices nationwide.
